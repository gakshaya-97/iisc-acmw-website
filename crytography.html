Securing the Future: A Journey into Cryptography

Date: 9 November 2024 — Time: 3:00 PM onwards — Venue: Room 117, CSA

Description

An introductory session on cryptography designed for newcomers. Broad overview of primitives, security arguments, secure multi-party computation, zero-knowledge proofs, and real-world applications. Includes toy problems and discussion of research directions.

Speaker

Arunachalaeshwaran V R — 3rd-year PhD, CSA, IISc (supervised by Dr. Bhavana Kanukurthi & Dr. Chaya Ganesh).

Session Highlights

  • Fundamentals of cryptographic primitives
  • Evolution and recent developments (MPC, ZK)
  • Real-world applications and research directions

Refreshments provided during breaks.
