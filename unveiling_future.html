Unveiling the Future — The Mathematics of Learning, Decision-Making, and Causal Inference

Date: 25 October 2024 — Time: 9:00 PM onwards — Venue: G01, Old Physics Building

Abstract

Explores three key areas behind recent advances in AI and LLMs: Learning Theory (sample complexity, VC dimension, bandits), Algorithmic Game Theory (Nash equilibria, market dynamics), and Causality (counterfactuals, Simpson’s paradox, graphical models). The talk connects theoretical foundations to practical research directions.

Speaker

Nirjhar Das — MTech (Research), IISc. Works on Computational Social Choice and Fairness under Prof. Siddharth Barman.

Prerequisites

Linear algebra, probability, and basic convex optimization — brief recaps will be provided during the talk.
